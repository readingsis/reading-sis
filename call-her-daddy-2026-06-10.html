TL;DR

Alex Cooper shares her deeply personal pregnancy journey, from the moment of discovery through navigating first-trimester anxiety and deciding when to go public. She reflects on how finding out she was pregnant forced her to confront her readiness for motherhood and manage the emotional weight of early pregnancy while maintaining her public persona.

About the show
›
Call Her Daddy is a podcast where host Alex Cooper explores relationships, sexuality, and personal growth through candid conversations. In this special solo episode, Alex opens up about her own life, sharing intimate details about her pregnancy journey and the emotional complexity of preparing for motherhood. The episode balances vulnerability with reflection, offering listeners a rare glimpse into the host's personal experiences.
Takeaways
1
**First-trimester secrecy compounds anxiety exponentially** Keeping early pregnancy private while experiencing acute anxiety creates a mental health crisis with no outlet. Alex couldn't discuss her fears with anyone until she was ready to go public, meaning she navigated some of the most vulnerable months entirely alone—a constraint that amplified rather than protected her emotional state.
2
**Public timing decisions reclaim agency in involuntary life changes** While pregnancy itself is imposed, Alex found power in controlling when and how to announce it publicly. This distinction—between the involuntary biological event and the voluntary disclosure—became a way to reassert autonomy and define her narrative on her terms.
3
**Unplanned pregnancies demand immediate emotional reckoning** When pregnancy doesn't arrive on a carefully laid timeline, the psychological shift is immediate and disorienting. Alex's experience shows that even high-agency individuals lose control in these moments, forcing a confrontation with identity, readiness, and autonomy that can't be postponed or managed away.
4
**Motherhood readiness emerges through lived experience, not planning** Alex's doubt about motherhood wasn't resolved through abstract consideration; it crystallized through the embodied experience of pregnancy itself. The physical and emotional journey became the proof point that changed her self-assessment from uncertain to certain.
5
**Telling your partner transforms pregnancy from personal to shared** Sharing pregnancy news with the father shifts it from private knowledge to relational reality, making it harder to contain emotionally or logistically. Alex found this moment harder than discovering the pregnancy itself because it required vulnerability with another person and invited their input on something deeply hers.
