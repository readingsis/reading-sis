Conan O'Brien Needs A Friend

Zach Galifianakis Returns Again

with Zach Galifianakis
4 May 2026 3 min read 1h 5m
TL;DR

Zach Galifianakis discusses how comedians have a responsibility to challenge power structures and authority, drawing on his early experiences in unconventional venues. He also reveals how his deep passion for gardening has evolved into a full creative show, reflecting his multi-faceted approach to performance and artistry.

Key Moments
Zach Galifianakis
“Being a busboy at a strip joint was like getting a PhD in human behavior—you see everything, you see people at their most vulnerable, and you learn what makes people tick.”
Discussing his early career experiences and what those formative jobs taught him about comedy and human nature
Zach Galifianakis
“Comics have to punch up, not down. If we're not making fun of the powerful, we're just reinforcing the existing power structure, and that's not what comedy should be doing.”
Explaining why it's crucial for comedians to target authority figures and institutions rather than marginalized groups
Conan O'Brien
“So you're telling me you went from strip clubs to making a gardening show—that's the most Zach Galifianakis arc I've ever heard.”
Reacting with amusement to Zach's seemingly unlikely pivot to creating This Is a Gardening Show
Zach Galifianakis
“Gardening is meditation, it's problem-solving, it's creativity all rolled into one. I realized I had to make a show about it because it's consumed my life in the best way possible.”
Explaining his deep personal connection to gardening and why he decided to develop it into a television project
Zach Galifianakis
“Being Conan's friend means you have to accept that he's funnier than you in most situations, but you show up anyway because the friendship is real.”
Joking about his relationship with Conan while reflecting on what genuine friendship in the comedy world means
About Zach Galifianakis
›
Zach Galifianakis is an acclaimed actor and comedian known for his distinctive comedic style and film roles including The Hangover franchise and Buster's Mal Heart. In this return visit to Conan's show, he reflects on his unconventional journey through comedy, including early work as a busboy at a strip joint. Beyond entertainment, Zach has channeled his passion for gardening into a new creative project, This Is a Gardening Show, blending his artistic sensibilities with horticultural pursuits.
Takeaways
1
Comedy's moral imperative to challenge power Zach emphasizes that comedians have a responsibility to critique those in authority rather than punch down at vulnerable populations. This philosophy shapes not just comedy content but the broader cultural impact of the medium, as comedians either reinforce or disrupt existing hierarchies through their material selection.
2
Unconventional early jobs build comedic insight Zach's experience as a busboy in a strip joint exposed him to raw human behavior and vulnerability, which he frames as essential education for understanding what makes comedy resonate. These formative, unglamorous jobs provided real-world material and emotional intelligence that later informed his comedy and performance work.
3
Passion projects can become professional ventures Zach's personal obsession with gardening evolved organically into This Is a Gardening Show, demonstrating how deep personal interests can be channeled into creative professional work. The transition shows how authenticity in passion can drive compelling content beyond traditional entertainment categories.
